The playfair cipher is a digraph substitution cipher. It employs a table where one letter of the alphabet is omitted, and the letters are arranged in a 5x5 grid. Encoded messages are broken down into two-letter chunks, and are swapped according to the grid. Repeated letters in the same chunk are usually separated by an X.
